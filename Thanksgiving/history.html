Thanksgiving History

Thanksgiving history begins with the 1621 harvest feast shared by the Pilgrims and the Wampanoag people in Plymouth. For over a century, observances were irregular. President George Washington issued the first national proclamation for a day of thanks in 1789. The holiday was made annual and national in 1863 when President Abraham Lincoln declared the last Thursday in November as Thanksgiving Day to unite the war-weary nation during the Civil War.
