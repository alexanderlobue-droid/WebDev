Celebrate the Season

Thanksgiving is a federal holiday in the United States, observed annually on the fourth Thursday of November. It originated from a 1621 harvest feast shared by the Plymouth colonists and the Wampanoag, and became a national holiday in 1863 when President Abraham Lincoln established the last Thursday of November as a day for giving thanks. The date was officially fixed by Congress in 1941 to ensure consistency. Modern traditions center around family gatherings, a large meal featuring turkey and seasonal dishes, watching parades and football games.
