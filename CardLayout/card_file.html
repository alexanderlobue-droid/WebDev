MLB Teams

New York Yankees

The New York Yankees are an American professional baseball team competing in MLB's American League East division. Established in 1901 in Baltimore and moving to New York in 1903, the Yankees have achieved unparalleled success, winning a record 27 World Series championships and 41 American League pennants. This legacy was built through contributions from legendary figures like Babe Ruth, Lou Gehrig, Joe DiMaggio, Mickey Mantle, Derek Jeter, and Mariano Rivera. They play their home games at Yankee Stadium in the Bronx, which opened in 2009.

Los Angeles Dodgers

The Los Angeles Dodgers are a Major League Baseball (MLB) team based in Los Angeles, California. Originally established in Brooklyn, New York, in 1883, they moved to Los Angeles in 1958. They hold 8 World Series titles and a record 25 National League (NL) pennants. Notable players include Jackie Robinson, who broke the MLB color barrier, Sandy Koufax, and current stars like Mookie Betts and Shohei Ohtani. Dodger Stadium, their home since 1962, is an iconic ballpark. The organization is also known for its strong community involvement through the Los Angeles Dodgers Foundation.

Philadelphia Phillies

The Philadelphia Phillies are a Major League Baseball (MLB) team based in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and compete in the National League (NL) East division. Founded in 1883, they are the oldest continuously operating, one-name, one-city franchise in American professional sports. The team's history has been marked by both struggles and successes, including a prolonged period with more losses than wins. Despite this, the Phillies have achieved significant milestones, including two World Series championships, in 1980 and 2008. They play their home games at Citizens Bank Park, which opened in 2004. Notable players throughout their history include Hall of Famers like Mike Schmidt and Steve Carlton, as well as more recent stars such as Bryce Harper and Aaron Nola.

Milwaukee Brewers

The Milwaukee Brewers are a Major League Baseball (MLB) team based in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, competing in the National League (NL) Central division. The franchise began as the Seattle Pilots in 1969 before relocating to Milwaukee in 1970. The team has made one World Series appearance in 1982, losing to the St. Louis Cardinals, and are one of five current MLB teams that have never won a World Series title. The Brewers play their home games at American Family Field. Notable players in their history include Hall of Famer Robin Yount, who played his entire career in Milwaukee from 1974 to 1993 and won two American League MVP awards. Other key players include Paul Molitor, Ryan Braun, and Christian Yelich, who won the National League MVP award in 2018. The Brewers have seen consistent playoff contention in recent years, winning the NL Central division in 2025.

Toronto Blue Jays

The Toronto Blue Jays are a Major League Baseball (MLB) team based in Toronto, Canada, and the only Canadian team currently competing in the league. They were founded in 1977 as an expansion franchise in the American League East division, where they continue to play today. While the franchise endured some typical growing pains in its early years, it rose to become a dominant force in the late 1980s and early 1990s, winning back-to-back World Series titles in 1992 and 1993 and becoming the first team outside of the United States to achieve that feat. After a period of struggle, the Blue Jays have re-emerged as perennial playoff contenders, driven by a roster of talented young stars like Vladimir Guerrero Jr. and Bo Bichette. They play their home games at Rogers Centre in downtown Toronto, a stadium known for its retractable roof and iconic location next to the CN Tower.
